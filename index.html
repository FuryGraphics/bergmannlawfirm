Frequently asked

Answers to the questions we hear most.

Don't see yours below? Call us at (210) 759-4336 — initial consultations are free.

What types of cases does the firm handle?

We focus on family law (divorce, custody, support, property division, adoption) and personal injury (car accidents, workplace injuries, wrongful death). We also take selected civil litigation and criminal defense matters.

How much does the initial consultation cost?

The first consultation is free. You'll meet with William, talk through the facts, and leave with a clear understanding of your options — no obligation.

How are fees structured?

Personal injury cases are typically handled on a contingency basis — you don't pay unless we recover for you. Family law and criminal matters are generally flat-fee or hourly, agreed in writing before we begin.

How long does a Texas divorce take?

Texas requires a 60-day waiting period from filing. Uncontested divorces often finalize shortly after; contested cases involving children or significant assets can take longer. We'll give you a realistic timeline at our first meeting.

What should I do right after a car accident?

Seek medical attention, call the police so there is a report, document the scene with photos, and exchange information. Then call us — before talking to the other driver's insurance company. What you say early can affect your recovery later.

Will I work directly with the attorney?

Yes. Every client works directly with William. You'll have his contact information and a clear plan for how and when you'll hear from him.
